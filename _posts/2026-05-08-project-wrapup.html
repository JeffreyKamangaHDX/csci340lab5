What skills and talents are most valuable in carrying out the social change work you are doing with your community partner? How well do those skills mesh with the talents or limitations you have come to see in yourself through the experience?

I feel that proper commmunication, being able to work with a team, and consistencey are most valuable in carrying out the social change work we was doing with our community partner.

I have had to work and play on many different teams with lots of different personalities, so I feel that working as a team for this project was an easy thing to do. The hardest part was probably consistencey with the work.

Luckily with a great team we were able to puck up at times when other members could not do as much. I think that is where communication played a great part in our team's success.

Have any of your fundamental values, social ideals, or significant beliefs been challenged or confirmed through this experience? Anything you learned in class that you would directly challenge now that you have had this experience?

One significant belief that has been confirmed is to treat people with respect ALWAYS. One big thing that FUMC stressed to us was to make sure that people feel comfortable asking for assistance. It is really hard to help people who do not want your help, and they won't want your help if you are not respectful.

Yes, I have found my academic coursework helpful in understanding what I have observed. This coursework has taught me how to be professional through communication, consistent with workload, while also understanding that anything can happen and you have to be able to adjust.
